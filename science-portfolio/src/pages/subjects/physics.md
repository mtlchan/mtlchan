## Overview

Physics explores the fundamental principles that govern the universe—from the smallest particles
to the largest galaxies. My physics classroom is a place where students develop problem-solving
skills and a deep understanding of how the world works.

## Curriculum

The physics course covers both classical and modern topics:

- **Mechanics** — motion, forces, energy, and momentum
- **Waves and optics** — sound, light, reflection, and refraction
- **Electricity and magnetism** — circuits, fields, and electromagnetism
- **Thermodynamics** — heat, temperature, and energy transfer
- **Modern physics** — relativity and quantum concepts (introductory)

## Demonstration Highlights

Physics lends itself to memorable classroom demonstrations:

- **Egg drop challenge** — applying impulse and momentum concepts
- **Standing wave demonstrations** — using ropes and Chladni plates
- **Van de Graaff generator** — exploring static electricity
- **Pendulum and projectile motion** — timing and predicting trajectories

## Lab Activities

Students engage in quantitative labs that build data analysis skills:

- Measuring gravitational acceleration with pendulums
- Building and analyzing series and parallel circuits
- Investigating Snell's law with refraction experiments
- Determining the speed of sound using resonance tubes

## Problem-Solving Focus

Physics develops critical thinking. Students regularly work through multi-step problems,
learning to break complex situations into manageable parts and apply fundamental principles.
