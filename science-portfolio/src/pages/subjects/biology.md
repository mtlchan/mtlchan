## Overview

Biology is the study of life in all its forms—from microscopic cells to vast ecosystems.
My courses guide students through the complexity and beauty of living systems, fostering
an appreciation for the natural world.

## Key Topics

The biology curriculum spans multiple levels of organization:

- **Cell biology** — structure, function, and cellular respiration
- **Genetics** — DNA, inheritance patterns, and gene expression
- **Evolution** — natural selection, adaptation, and speciation
- **Ecology** — ecosystems, food webs, and environmental science
- **Human anatomy** — organ systems and physiology

## Hands-On Learning

### Microscopy Labs

Students learn to prepare slides and use compound microscopes to observe:

- Onion root tip mitosis
- Pond water microorganisms
- Leaf stomata and chloroplasts

### Field Studies

When weather permits, we take biology outside. Students conduct biodiversity surveys
on the school grounds, identifying plant and animal species and analyzing ecosystem relationships.

### Dissections

For students who opt in, dissection labs provide a tangible understanding of anatomy.
Specimens include frog, earthworm, and flower dissections.

## Student Projects

Each semester, students complete an independent research project on a topic of their choice,
from local water quality testing to studies on antibiotic resistance.
