## Overview

Archaeology brings history to life by uncovering the physical traces of past civilizations.
Through hands-on fieldwork, students learn how scientists piece together the story of
human history—from ancient tools to buried settlements.

## Pennyhooks Archaeology

Our recent fieldwork at Pennyhooks gave students a taste of real archaeological investigation.
Working alongside professional archaeologists, students participated in:

- **Site surveying** — mapping and documenting the excavation area
- **Stratigraphy observation** — reading soil layers to understand chronological sequences
- **Artefact recovery** — careful excavation, recording, and preservation of finds
- **Post-excavation analysis** — cleaning, cataloguing, and interpreting recovered items

## What Students Learned

Beyond the excitement of discovery, the experience reinforced key concepts:

- **Scientific method in practice** — forming hypotheses about site usage and testing them
- **Interdisciplinary connections** — archaeology draws on chemistry (material analysis),
  biology (environmental context), and physics (dating techniques)
- **Patience and precision** — good archaeology requires meticulous attention to detail
- **Teamwork** — excavations are collaborative efforts where every role matters
