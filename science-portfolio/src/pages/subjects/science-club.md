## Overview

The Science Club is an after-school program for students who want to explore science
beyond the curriculum. It's a space for creativity, competition, and collaboration—
where students drive their own learning through projects of their choosing.

## Mission

To provide a welcoming environment where any student, regardless of background
or ability, can discover the joy of scientific exploration and develop teamwork
and leadership skills.

## Activities

### Science Fair Preparation

Club members receive mentoring as they develop science fair projects. We cover
topic selection, experimental design, poster creation, and presentation skills.
Several members have advanced to regional competitions.

### STEM Challenges

Regular engineering challenges that encourage creative problem-solving:

- Bridge building (toothpicks and glue — maximum load wins)
- Water rocket design and launch
- Solar oven construction
- Egg drop vehicles

### Guest Speakers

We invite scientists, engineers, and medical professionals to speak about their
careers and the paths that led them there. Past speakers have included a
marine biologist, a chemical engineer, and a science journalist.

### Citizen Science

Club members participate in real scientific research through citizen science projects,
including bird population surveys and light pollution monitoring.

## Join Us

The Science Club meets every Thursday after school in Room 204. All students are welcome,
and no prior experience is needed—just curiosity.
