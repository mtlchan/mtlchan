## Overview

The laboratory is where science comes alive. Lab activities are designed to build
practical skills, reinforce theoretical concepts, and—above all—cultivate a spirit
of inquiry and discovery.

## Safety First

All lab activities begin with a thorough review of safety protocols:

- Proper use of personal protective equipment (goggles, gloves, lab coats)
- Safe handling of glassware, chemicals, and heating equipment
- Emergency procedures and first-aid locations
- Waste disposal guidelines

## Lab Skills Developed

Throughout the year, students progressively build these skills:

- **Measurement and precision** — using balances, graduated cylinders, and pipettes
- **Observation and documentation** — keeping detailed lab notebooks
- **Experimental design** — identifying variables, controls, and sample sizes
- **Data analysis** — graphing, statistical interpretation, error analysis
- **Scientific communication** — writing formal lab reports

## Featured Labs

### Chemistry: Acid-Base Titration

Students determine the concentration of an unknown acid using a standardized base
and phenolphthalein indicator. Emphasis on technique and precision.

### Biology: Enzyme Activity

Investigating the effect of temperature and pH on catalase activity using potato
extracts and hydrogen peroxide.

### Physics: Ohm's Law Investigation

Building circuits with resistors, measuring voltage and current, and verifying
Ohm's law through graphical analysis.

### Cross-Disciplinary: Water Quality Analysis

Students collect water samples from local sources and test for pH, dissolved oxygen,
nitrates, and turbidity—combining chemistry, biology, and environmental science.
