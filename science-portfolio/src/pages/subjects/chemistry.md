## Overview

Chemistry is the study of matter—what substances are made of, how they interact, and how they change.
In my classroom, students explore everything from atomic structure to organic reactions through
a mix of theory and hands-on experimentation.

## Classroom Approach

My chemistry curriculum emphasizes inquiry-based learning. Students are encouraged to ask questions,
form hypotheses, and test them in the lab. Key topics covered include:

- Atomic structure and the periodic table
- Chemical bonding and molecular geometry
- Stoichiometry and reaction balancing
- Acids, bases, and pH
- Thermochemistry and reaction rates
- Introduction to organic chemistry

## Lab Highlights

> "Tell me and I forget. Teach me and I remember. Involve me and I learn." — Benjamin Franklin

Labs are central to the chemistry experience. Students regularly conduct experiments such as:

- **Titration labs** — determining unknown concentrations
- **Flame tests** — identifying metal ions by color
- **Synthesis of esters** — making pleasant-smelling compounds
- **Electrolysis of water** — splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen

## Student Work Examples

Students produce lab reports, molecular models, and research posters throughout the year.
Their work is displayed in the classroom and at school science fairs.
